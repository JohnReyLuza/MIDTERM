Oroquieta, originally known as Layawan, is a component city and the capital of Misamis Occidental, Philippines. Located in the northern part of the province, it serves as the center of government, trade, and commerce. The city is known for its scenic coastal views, agricultural industries, and rich cultural heritage.

According to the 2020 census, Oroquieta has a population of 72,301 people. The city plays a significant role in the province's economy, with industries such as farming, fishing, and small-scale manufacturing contributing to its growth. It is also home to various educational institutions and government offices that cater to the needs of its residents.

Oroquieta is a peaceful and progressive city that continues to develop while maintaining its natural beauty and historical significance.
